# Examples

Worked examples of the Common Thread methodology applied to specific investigations will appear here in future versions. v1 ships without a case study.

## Why v1 has no case study

The methodology paper's §9 reserves a slot for a worked example but does not fill it. Two reasons:

1. **Anonymization is its own research problem.** Methodology case studies involving real coordinated-behavior networks require redacting identities, relationships, and platform artifacts in ways that preserve the methodology's evidentiary points without exposing real people. The redaction discipline is nontrivial and is not solved in v1.

2. **The author's own case is the wrong v1 example.** The methodology was developed during an active state-court matter. Using that matter as the v1 case study would conflate the methodology's general utility with the author's specific situation, which §10.2 and §11 of the paper explicitly guard against.

## What a case study should contain

A worked example here should include:

- The seed set with basis statements per the §3 evidentiary framework
- The signals collected and the extractors applied per §4 and §6
- The attribution reasoner's output with citations per §7
- A reflection on the methodology's strengths and limits in that case
- Anonymization notes documenting what was redacted and why

Identifiers (account handles, real names, employer information, geographic specifics finer than city level) must be replaced with stable pseudonyms. Relationships between pseudonyms must be preserved so the methodology's claims remain checkable. Where anonymization is impossible without breaking the evidentiary chain, the case is not a suitable example here.
